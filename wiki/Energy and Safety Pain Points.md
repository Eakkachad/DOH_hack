# Energy and Safety Pain Points

This document synthesizes the core challenges the [[Department of Highways]] (DOH) faces regarding energy consumption, budget constraints, and road safety. These pain points are particularly relevant for initiatives like the [[DOH Hackathon 2026]] focusing on "Sustainable & Low-Carbon Highway" solutions.

## 1. The True Scale of the Energy Bill
- **Decentralized Data:** The highway electricity budget is highly fragmented across 104 Highway Districts and approximately 7,850 Local Administrative Organizations (LAOs). This makes it difficult for the DOH to have a clear, unified dashboard of energy costs.
- **Massive Cost:** Despite the fragmentation, recent extrapolations estimate the true national highway electricity bill is **over 1.7 billion THB annually**. 

## 2. Policy vs. Safety Conflict (The "Lights-Off" Dilemma)
- **The Policy:** To cut costs, the DOH enacted a policy (starting around May 2026) to turn off streetlights on 4-digit secondary highways from 22:00 to 06:00. This saves roughly **1,600 THB per night** for a 10km stretch.
- **The VSL Reality:** The [[Value of a Statistical Life (VSL)]] in Thailand is calculated between **17.2 million and 39.9 million THB** per road fatality. 
- **The Conflict:** If reduced visibility from the lights-off policy causes just *one* additional fatal accident, the macroeconomic loss entirely wipes out the electricity savings for that 10km stretch for the next 30 to 68 years. This proves that crude cost-cutting measures are economically inviable when they compromise safety.

## 3. The 2040 LED Transition Bottlenecks
- **The Goal:** The DOH aims for an 80% LED transition (or Solar LED) by 2040 to achieve a Low-Carbon Highway network.
- **The Roadblocks:** Progress is heavily bottlenecked by:
  - Rigid e-bidding procurement rules.
  - High median prices (e.g., 18,000 THB for a 120W ULTRABRIGHT LED set).
  - The "K-Value liquidity crisis" (unfunded arrears to contractors), which freezes CapEx budgets.
- **Alternative Needs:** Because the DOH lacks upfront capital, there is a critical need for alternative business models such as Energy Performance Contracts (EPC) or Hardware-as-a-Service (HaaS).

## 4. 24/7 Energy Drain from ITS Assets
- **The Load:** Unlike streetlights that only run at night, Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS) operate 24/7. 
- **The Scale:** Nationally, there are roughly **750 VMS boards** (which draw heavy power during the day to combat glare) and **5,000+ CCTV cameras** (requiring constant PoE and server processing).
- **Total Consumption:** These assets consume an estimated **14.23 GWh annually**. Transitioning them to off-grid solar micro-grids is a high-priority opportunity.
