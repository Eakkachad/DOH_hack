# Source: Strategic Assessment of the Department of Highways Thailand

## Summary
A comprehensive research report analyzing the Department of Highways' (DOH) energy expenditures, the macroeconomic impact of road safety (specifically the "lights-off" policy), procurement bottlenecks, and the 2040 Low-Carbon Infrastructure transition. The report mathematically extrapolates the true national energy bill to over 1.7 billion THB and argues that the economic cost of nighttime traffic fatalities vastly outweighs the utility savings from turning off streetlights.

## Bullet Points
- **Decentralized Energy Bill**: The national highway electricity bill is fragmented across 104 Highway Districts and ~7,850 Local Administrative Organizations (LAOs). The true cost is extrapolated to be over 1.7 billion THB annually.
- **The "Lights-Off" Policy**: Enacted on May 1, 2026, turning off streetlights on 4-digit highways between 22:00 and 06:00. This saves ~1,600 THB per night per 10km, but a single fatality costs the economy between 17.2M and 39.9M THB.
- **2040 Low Carbon Highway**: Goal to reach 80% LED transition by 2040. However, progress is bottlenecked by rigid e-bidding rules, high median prices (e.g., 18,000 THB for a 120W ULTRABRIGHT LED set), and the K-Value liquidity crisis (unfunded arrears to contractors).
- **Alternative Procurement**: Proposes Energy Performance Contracts (EPC) or Hardware-as-a-Service (HaaS) to bypass CapEx limitations.
- **ITS Micro-grids**: Extrapolates ~750 VMS boards and ~5,000+ CCTV cameras nationally, consuming ~14.23 GWh annually. Proposes transitioning low-draw ITS assets (like CCTVs) to off-grid solar micro-grids.
