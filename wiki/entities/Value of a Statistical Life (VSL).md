# Value of a Statistical Life (VSL) in Thailand

**Value of a Statistical Life (VSL)**, in the context of Thailand's road network, is the macroeconomic valuation of a human life lost in a traffic accident, factoring in lost productivity, medical costs, property damage, and systemic healthcare burdens.

## Macroeconomic Cost
According to the Thailand Development Research Institute (TDRI) and DOH internal studies, the aggregate economic loss from road fatalities is catastrophic:
- **Baseline Estimate (TDRI/UN)**: ~100 billion THB annually (approx. 1% of GDP), translating to at least **17.2 million THB per fatality**.
- **Comprehensive DOH Estimate**: ~232 billion THB annually (2.8% of GDP). Based on 2023 crash data (5,807 fatalities), the true macroeconomic cost is estimated at **39.9 million THB per fatal incident**.

## Clash with Energy Austerity
The VSL drastically invalidates short-term cost-cutting measures like the DOH's May 2026 "lights-off" policy (turning off streetlights on secondary highways from 22:00 to 06:00). 
- A typical 10km stretch of highway saves roughly 584,000 THB annually by turning off lights. 
- If reduced visibility causes just **one** additional fatal accident, the macroeconomic loss (17.2M - 39.9M THB) eclipses the electricity savings for the next 30 to 68 years.

## Vulnerable Demographics
Removing artificial lighting disproportionately endangers specific groups:
- **Motorcyclists**: Account for ~74.5% of road deaths.
- **The Elderly**: Fatalities rising at 5.45% annually due to diminished nocturnal visual acuity.
- **Pedestrians**: Average over 30 deaths per month.
