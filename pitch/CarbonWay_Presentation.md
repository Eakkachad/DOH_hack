## Appendix A: Carbon Calculation — Full Math

### 1. Emission Factor (แหล่งอ้างอิง)
```
Thailand Grid Emission Factor (TGO / อบก.): 0.4999 kgCO₂e/kWh
Reference: Thailand Greenhouse Gas Management Organization, official GHG database
```

### 2. ITS Assets Baseline (ข้อมูลจริง)
```
VMS Boards:     750 × 2.0 kW × 12h × 365  =   6,570,000 kWh/year
CCTV Cameras:   5,000 × 0.1 kW × 24h × 365 =  4,380,000 kWh/year
Servers/Network (estimated PoE + processing) = 3,280,000 kWh/year
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Total ITS:                                    14,230,000 kWh/year
Carbon: 14,230,000 × 0.4999 ÷ 1,000 =         ~7,114 tCO₂e/year

T-VER Revenue (30% reducible via solar):
  2,134 tCO₂e × 100 THB = 213,400 THB/year (conservative)
  2,134 tCO₂e × 200 THB = 426,800 THB/year (optimistic)
```

### 3. Streetlights Baseline (ประมาณการ)
```
DOE Estimate: ~200,000 lights × 0.15kW avg × 10h/night × 365
            = 109,500,000 kWh/year
```

## Appendix C: T-VER — Mechanism, Market & Precedent

### What is T-VER?
**Thailand Voluntary Emission Reduction Program**  
Administered by **TGO** (องค์การบริหารจัดการก๊าซเรือนกระจก / Thailand Greenhouse Gas Management Organization) since 2013.

### How T-VER Works
```
Project Developer → Register project with TGO
                 → Establish baseline emissions
                 → Implement reduction activities
                 → Monitor & verify reductions
                 → TGO issues T-VER credits (1 credit = 1 tCO₂e reduced)
                 → Sell credits on carbon market
                 → Revenue used for further reductions
```

### CarbonWay's T-VER Pathway
```
DOH Highway Assets → Electricity Consumption → CO₂ Emissions (baseline)
                                                      ↓
                   LED / Solar / Smart Dimming Intervention
                                                      ↓
                   Measurable tCO₂e Reduction (verified by TGO methodology)
                                                      ↓
                   T-VER Credits Issued → Sold → Revenue → Reinvest
```

### Carbon Price Trajectory

| Year | Price (THB/tCO₂e) | Market Driver |
|------|-------------------|---------------|
| 2026 | 100 – 200 | Current voluntary market |
| 2027-2028 | 200 – 300 | Thailand carbon tax deliberation (Excise Dept.) |
| 2029-2030 | 300 – 500 | Mandatory ETS (Emission Trading Scheme) expected |
| 2030+ | 500+ | CBAM (EU Carbon Border Adjustment) spillover effect |

### Existing T-VER Precedent in Thailand
Multiple Thai government agencies and state enterprises have successfully registered T-VER projects, including energy efficiency, renewable energy, and waste management projects. This proves the mechanism is accessible to DOH.
